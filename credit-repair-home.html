Credit Repair

Our firm uses several tools to help consumers repair the credit by utilizing several legal tools at its arsenal designed by Federal laws to protect consumers and their rights. One such tool is the Fair Credit Repair Reporting Act (FCRA) gives consumers the right to correct or delete inaccurate or unverifiable information from their credit reports.

The Credit Repair Process at a Glance:

Intake

We will assist you to obtain your credit report from all major credit reporting bureaus. Since each case is unique we will gather specific information regarding the circumstances for each item in question.

Credit Review & Analysis

Our attorneys and paralegals will then start formulating the best viable credit repair strategy for your specific circumstance.

Service & Follow Up

Once we have conducted a proper analysis and determined the most appropriate strategy we will rigorously advocate your best interest for the best possible outcome.

We do this by using a variety of laws to demand full verification of the source, validity and legal authority of the debt collector to pursue you by reporting disputed information in an attempt to remove wrongful negative or misleading information reflected on your credit.

Often times the creditor in question either does not respond or is ill equipped to provide factual data and evidence needed in order to prove the debt.

These firms often prey on unsuspecting consumers who do not know their rights.

In short, we ask them to prove the debt by providing the evidence that is required and the burden of proof showing the original documents indicating that you have either done business with them, signed a contract or provide the basis for the negative entry. If they are unable to do within legal guidelines by law they have to remove the negative entry or be subject to fines under Federal Fair Credit Reporting Act or FCRA.

Legal Action

If the creditors are uncooperative or unwilling to delete, remove, alter or update any negative entry or reporting that is not compliant with the laws such as Federal Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) we will analyze your case for possible legal action against the alleged creditor or entity who may be reporting the negative line item to the credit bureaus.

We know and understand consumer laws and know how to best prosecute violations by creditors and debt collectors.
